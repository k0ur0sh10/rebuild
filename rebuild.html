THE RULES

The plan isn't the hard part — these are. Read them when you're about to break the chain, beat yourself up, or escalate too fast.

1
Minimums are minimums, not targets.

"One exercise," "25 minutes," "first 3 morning steps" — built to be too small to fail. The size is the strategy. You're casting a vote for "I follow through," not chasing a perfect day.

2
A missed step is data, not a verdict.

THE one for you. You fear free will because a slip "fucks with your self-esteem." The fix isn't removing choice — it's experiencing, over and over, that you can miss a step and the building stands.

3
When you slip: next rep, no beating.

You WILL miss days. The skill that changes your life is the boring return: "Missed it. Noted. Tomorrow I don't." The shame spiral is the real relapse — it costs more days than the slip.

4
Guilt is not accountability — it's burned fuel.

Calling yourself a fraud feels like a standard. It's not. It drains the energy you need to act → couch more appealing → more shame. Drop the megaphone, keep the signal.

5
Don't escalate until the floor is automatic.

The second flow returns, you'll want to stack ten habits and go monk mode. That perfectionism caused the binge. Hold Phase 1 the full 30 days. Flow is downstream of consistency, not intensity.

6
Detail the bookends, flex the middle.

Morning and night get step-level detail because they're yours to control. The midday stays flexible because real life is unpredictable. Over-scripting the middle is just a finer stick to hit yourself with.

7
Protect the 7:00 wake above all.

It's the lever that holds the week. Skip everything else before you skip this. A held wake time after a bad night beats a perfect day after a slept-in one.

WHEN I SLIP

Open this the moment you've broken the chain, numbed, or feel the shame spiral starting. Run the protocol instead of the beating.

1 — Say the boring sentence

"I slipped. Noted. The next rep is the only thing that matters now." No "I always do this." No character audit. The slip is one data point; the spiral does the real damage.

2 — Name what you were escaping

You didn't numb because you're weak — you did it to escape a feeling. Boredom (likely, with no structure right now)? Work felt threatening? Self-hatred you were muting? Name it:

3 — Shrink the comeback to absurd

Do NOT plan a perfect day to "make up for it" — that's the swing that snaps. Smallest possible next rep: one glass of water, shoes on, open one file for 5 min. The tiny thing?

4 — The streak was never the point

That number is a tool, not the goal. The goal is being a man who returns fast. A reset streak with a fast return is a win. Reset without ceremony, take your tiny rep.
